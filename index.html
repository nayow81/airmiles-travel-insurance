Did you know?

The Government Health Insurance Plan (GHIP) only pays a small portion of medical bills incurred outside of Canada, which means that travel medical expenses can be very expensive.

The Travel Health Insurance Association of Canada recommends you follow these three “travel rules” before you leave the country.

Travel rule #1: Know your health

Do you have any pre-existing conditions? Consult your healthcare provider if you’re not sure.

Travel rule #2: Know your trip

How long is your trip? Is your family joining you? Will you be doing any sporting or adventure activities?

Travel rule #3: Know your policy

What am I covered for? Are pre-existing conditions covered? What is the emergency contact number? What are the policy benefits?

Travel insurance is designed to cover losses arising from sudden and unforeseeable circumstances. It is important that you read and understand your policy before you travel as your coverage may be subject to certain exclusions or limitations. A pre-existing condition exclusion may apply to medical conditions and/or symptoms that existed prior to your trip. Frequently asked questions

How will the charge for the insurance policy appear on my credit card statement?

The charge will appear as AIR MILES® Travel Insurance.

How will the charge for the insurance policy appear on my credit card statement?

Only travel (flight, car, hotel) booked on airmiles.ca or with an AIR MILES Customer Care specialist is eligible for coverage with AIR MILES Travel Insurance.

I booked multiple travel products, using Miles. Can I insure them all under one policy?

You can select and purchase an insurance policy that suits your needs once you have finished all of your bookings. During this process you can insure the full value of your trip. Conditions, limitations and exclusions apply. More details will be provided on your emailed travel itinerary.

What is family coverage?

Family coverage is available to you if all family members to be insured under one policy are named in your confirmation, are under age 60, and you have purchased and paid for family coverage. The family coverage covers you, a member of your family (spouse, parent or adult son or daughter travelling with you) and your child(ren) or grandchild(ren). To be eligible for insurance under a plan that includes Emergency Medical coverage, the child(ren) or grandchild(ren) must be at least 31 days of age. Note: A maximum of 2 adults is permitted under family coverage. Conditions, limitations and exclusions apply.

I have already booked my trip on airmiles.ca. Can I purchase travel insurance for my AIR MILES bookings?

Yes, you can add insurance coverage by visiting AIR MILES Travel Insurance.

  • Prince Edward Island

  • Saskatchewan

I booked a one-way flight using Miles but I am staying in the destination for a longer time period. Can I book insurance for my entire trip?

You can select and purchase an insurance policy that suits your needs after you have finished your booking. During this process you will indicate your extended return date. Conditions, limitations and exclusions apply. More details will be provided on your emailed travel itinerary.

For more information on the AIR MILES Travel Insurance policy, click here.

I'm travelling with an infant. Do I need to purchase a separate policy for the infant?

If you have an infant who is at least 31 days old and under the age of 2 years who is an immediate family member, travelling with you and listed on the confirmation for this insurance, the infant will be covered at no charge under your policy if you have purchased the Worldwide Comprehensive Plan or the Canada Comprehensive Plan.
